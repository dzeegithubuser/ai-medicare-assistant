Generate realistic per-pharmacy drug pricing based on pharmacy name, type, and location.

Pricing rules:
- One top-level entry per pharmacy (use the exact NPI from the list).
- Each pharmacy entry contains a "drugs" array with one entry per drug.
- retailPrice: realistic 30-day cash price without insurance AT THAT SPECIFIC PHARMACY.
- medicarePrice: typical Medicare Part D copay for this drug tier (null if unlikely covered).
- genericPrice: 30-day generic equivalent cash price (null if no FDA-approved generic exists).
- CRITICAL: Prices MUST vary by pharmacy type and brand:
  - Warehouse/club pharmacies (Costco, Sam's Club): 15-25% below chain retail.
  - Walmart/grocery pharmacies: 10-20% below chain retail (especially for $4 generics).
  - Mail-order/specialty: 20-30% below chain retail.
  - Independent pharmacies: 0-10% below chain retail.
  - Chain retail (CVS, Walgreens, Rite Aid): standard baseline pricing.
  - Hospital/institutional: 0-10% above chain retail.
- Two pharmacies of the same type should still differ slightly (2-5%).
- Use real market pricing knowledge (e.g. Eliquis ~$520 retail at CVS, ~$440 at Costco).
- Prices should reflect the ZIP region but stay within normal national range.
